Caesar Cipher

Description

Julius Caesar employed the Caesar cipher, a straightforward encryption method, to communicate with his supporters in secret. The "shift" or "key" is the number of positions by which the plaintext message's letters are shifted. One of the earliest and most basic encryption schemes is the Caesar Cypher.

It is essentially a kind of substitution cipher, in which every letter in a given text is swapped out for a letter that is located a certain number of positions lower in the alphabet. With a shift of one, for instance, B would take the place of A, B would become C, and so on. Julius Caesar is said to have utilized the technique to communicate with his officials, which is why it bears his name.
